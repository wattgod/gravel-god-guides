The G Spot (88-92% FTP)

This is gravel race pace. Hard enough to move fast and stay with groups. Sustainable enough to hold for extended periods. This is where you'll spend most of your race.

Why "G Spot"? Because it hits the spot—maximum sustainable power for gravel racing.

6. Equipment

Equipment Checklist

Bike:

  • Tires: Correct width, fresh rubber, tested 2+ weeks
  • Sealant: Topped up within 2 weeks
  • Drivetrain: Clean, lubed, shifting perfectly
  • Brakes: Pads checked, rotors clean
  • Fit: Comfortable for 6+ hours

Kit:

  • Chamois: Tested on long rides
  • Jersey: Race-day specific (not new)
  • Weather layers: As needed for forecast

Nutrition:

  • On-bike food: Race-proven only
  • Hydration: Bottles filled, mix tested
  • Emergency calories: Extra gel in jersey

Tools:

  • Multitool with chain breaker
  • Spare tube or tire plug kit
  • CO2 or mini pump

8. Altitude Preparation

Racing at 11,000 feet means your power will be 12-18% lower than at sea level.

Your Options

StrategyWhenBest For
Arrive Early10-21 days beforeMaximum adaptation, if schedule allows
Arrive Late<24 hours beforeAvoid acute symptoms, limited adaptation
Altitude Block3-4 weeks out, return 2 weeks beforePeak adaptation + sea-level sharpening
No Altitude Access?
Heat training provides some altitude crossover benefits. Prioritize that if altitude exposure isn't possible.

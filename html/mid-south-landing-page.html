TL;DR

The Mid South is gravel racing's season opener and biggest personality cult. When it's dry (recent trend), you get a blazingly fast 100-mile tactical race on hard-packed red clay with average speeds over 20mph for the front groups. When it rains (historically common), those same roads become unrideable peanut butter mud that destroys drivetrains and forces miles of hike-a-bike. Either way, you finish to a hug from race director Bobby Wintle, live music, and the kind of community vibe that makes people come back year after year despite the suffering.

You should race this if: You want the ultimate gravel season opener test against stacked pro fields, you can handle extreme weather variability, and you value atmosphere/community as much as competition.

⚠️ Skip this if:

You need predictable conditions, hate tactical pack racing, or want a remote self-supported adventure instead of a festival atmosphere.

The Ratings

Prestige
5/5 - Season opener status, $100K purse, Bobby Wintle legend, 4,000+ field, attracts WorldTour pros
Length
3/5 - 100 miles is substantial but raceable. Fast times (4:26-4:47 for winners). 50-mile option available.
Technicality
5/5 - Creek crossings, singletrack, red clay chaos. Dry: 3.5/5. Mud: 5/5 (bike-destroying peanut butter).
Elevation
2/5 - 3,500ft over 100 miles. Rolling "shark teeth" terrain with constant small climbs. Not mountainous.
Climate
5/5 - THE defining variable. March Oklahoma = anything goes. Recent: snow, wildfires, perfect conditions. Red clay + rain = race-ending.
Altitude
1/5 - Stillwater at ~900ft elevation. Essentially sea level racing. No altitude concerns.
Adventure Factor
2/5 (dry) / 5/5 (mud) - Mass start with aid stations. But when mud hits, it's pure survival chaos.
Overall Score: 72/100

Elite Regional Classic territory. Must-do for competitive gravel racers, especially those focused on tactical racing rather than ultra-endurance. The 5/5 climate score means this race can play significantly harder than 72 suggests.
